---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of evaluating and ranking tasks, goals, ideas, or initiatives so limited time and capacity are directed to the highest-value work first. It turns broad goals and roadmap items into a clear sequence of action by weighing factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, readiness, effort, and available resources. In practice, it means deciding what should be done next, what can wait, and how to make tradeoffs explicit when demands exceed capacity. Effective prioritization uses a consistent framework rather than intuition alone, helping teams compare competing work more objectively, stay aligned on desired outcomes, and avoid scattered effort. It is especially useful when multiple streams of work must be balanced at once, such as content, architecture, release planning, roadmap decisions, backlog grooming, milestone sequencing, development planning, and other improvement work.
