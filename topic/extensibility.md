---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so new capabilities can be added over time with minimal disruption to existing behavior. It relies on clear boundaries, stable core abstractions, separation of concerns, and intentional extension points or interfaces that create practical seams for future growth. The goal is to support safe evolution without major restructuring, reducing coupling and preserving the clarity, control, and maintainability of the core system as requirements change. Extensibility should be treated as a core design goal, not an afterthought, and it applies broadly to technical systems, content models, and other evolving architectures. Plugin-based approaches can implement extensibility, but the concept is broader than plugins alone.
