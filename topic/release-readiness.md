---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, preparation steps, and checkpoints used to determine whether software, modules, or content are ready to ship. It focuses on ensuring that work is complete enough, reviewed, packaged, validated, and coordinated so the release is safe, polished, and aligned with expectations. In practice, release readiness uses checklists, milestones, and readiness reviews to reduce surprises at launch, confirm that changes meet a defined standard, and support predictable release decisions. It also acts as a bridge between planning and delivery by translating roadmap items into release-ready scope, clarifying priorities within the current release window, and deferring work that is not sufficiently complete or feasible. Rather than a one-time approval gate, it is an ongoing part of the development process and a practical standard for launch decisions.
