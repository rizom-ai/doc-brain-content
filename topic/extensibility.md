---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the design principle of building systems so they can grow and adapt over time without major rewrites. It depends on modular architecture, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, loose coupling, and a stable core with well-defined extension points. Good extensibility is planned from the start: the core stays protected while new capabilities, integrations, behaviors, or plugins can be added in controlled ways. Flexible boundaries and replaceable or expandable components reduce the cost of future change and help the system remain maintainable. The goal is long-term adaptability, predictable evolution, and the ability to add new functionality without losing clarity or structural integrity.
