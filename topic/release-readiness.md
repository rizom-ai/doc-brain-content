---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is ready to ship. It defines the conditions that should be met before delivery, connecting planning with execution so teams know what completion looks like and when a release can safely proceed. Readiness typically includes verifying required functionality, confirming quality and reliability expectations, validating key requirements, resolving dependencies and blockers, and ensuring launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. In more complex products, such as those with plugins or other integration points, release-readiness checks also help surface compatibility and stability risks. Release reviews, checklists, and go-live gates provide a practical decision point between development and deployment, helping teams avoid rushed or ad hoc delivery, reduce operational risk, and make confident decisions about timing and scope.
