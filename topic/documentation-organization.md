---
title: Documentation Organization
---
This topic covers the planning and structuring of documentation or knowledge bases so information remains easy to navigate, understand, and maintain as it grows. It includes deciding how to group related material, define consistent conventions, connect notes or pages, and create a clear hierarchy that supports discovery, reuse, and coherent browsing. The focus is on making the system feel organized rather than fragmented, reducing duplication and confusion, and helping both current and new contributors understand the structure quickly. It is especially relevant when building or refining a documentation framework beyond a simple list of files or pages, where long-term maintainability and usability depend on strong structure.
