---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is complete, stable, and safe enough to ship. It provides a practical go/no-go framework for assessing release-bound work consistently before deployment or publication. Common readiness factors include confirming that required work is complete, key conditions are verified, features are tested and meet quality expectations, scope and priorities align with the release goal or next milestone, risks and blockers are understood or resolved, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. A release readiness checklist helps teams verify these requirements before launch and creates a more predictable delivery process. The topic is closely tied to coordination between development, QA, release planning, and support, and it should be built into the workflow rather than treated as an afterthought. As a final quality and coordination checkpoint, it connects planning to execution and turns prioritized work into a shippable outcome.
