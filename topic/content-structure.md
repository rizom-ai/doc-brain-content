---
title: Content Structure
---
Content structure is the underlying framework used to arrange documentation, knowledge base topics, pages, sections, and related content artifacts. It defines how information is grouped, ordered, and linked so that content remains clear, consistent, navigable, and reusable. A strong structure makes it easier for readers to scan and understand information, and it gives authors a shared pattern for creating examples, best practices, and getting-started material without fragmentation. It also supports scalability and maintainability by reducing duplication, preserving coherence as content grows, and relying on stable organizing principles such as hierarchy, naming conventions, sequencing, relationship patterns, and reusable formats. Because content systems evolve, structure should be reviewed and adjusted intentionally to keep the overall system usable for both contributors and readers.
