---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so new capabilities can be added over time with minimal disruption to existing behavior. It depends on modular design, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, stable core abstractions, and intentional extension points or interfaces that provide safe seams for future growth. The goal is to keep the base system stable while allowing new features or outside components to integrate cleanly, reducing coupling and preserving maintainability as requirements change. Extensibility should be treated as a core design goal rather than an afterthought, and it applies broadly to software, content models, and other evolving architectures. Plugin-based approaches can support extensibility, but the concept is broader than plugins alone.
