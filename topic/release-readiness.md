---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparation steps used to determine whether software, modules, or content are ready to ship. It ensures work is complete enough, reviewed, validated, packaged, and aligned with expectations before launch. Typical readiness practices include checklists, milestone reviews, and final validation to confirm completeness, accuracy, formatting or packaging, consistency, and fit with the current release scope. It serves as an ongoing quality gate throughout the workflow rather than a one-time approval, helping prevent incomplete or inconsistent material from reaching users and supporting safe, predictable release decisions.
