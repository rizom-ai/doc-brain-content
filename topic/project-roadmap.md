---
title: Project Roadmap
---
A project roadmap is a high-level, time-based plan that turns strategy, priorities, and incoming ideas into a sequenced path of work. It organizes deliverables, milestones, dependencies, and release timing over a defined horizon such as a quarter or several months, helping teams decide what should happen next, what can wait, and how work should be staged for maximum impact. Roadmaps are especially useful when resources are limited or goals shift, because they connect near-term execution with longer-term direction.

The focus is on planning and prioritization rather than detailed task management. A strong roadmap supports tradeoff decisions, coordination across product and technical efforts, and clear communication to stakeholders about scope, expectations, and delivery timing. It should provide enough structure to guide commitments while remaining adaptable as priorities, constraints, and opportunities change.
