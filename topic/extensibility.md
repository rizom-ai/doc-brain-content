---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so new capabilities can be added over time with minimal disruption to existing behavior. It depends on modular design, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, stable core abstractions, and well-defined extension points or interfaces that create safe seams for growth. A well-extensible system keeps its core stable while allowing features, integrations, and components to evolve independently, making change predictable and controlled rather than requiring major rewrites. Extensibility should be considered early in design to support long-term adaptability across software systems, content models, and other evolving architectures.
