---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the design principle of building systems so they can grow and adapt without major rewrites. It relies on modular architecture, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, loose coupling, and a stable core with well-defined extension points. Good extensibility is planned from the start: the core remains protected while new capabilities, integrations, behaviors, or plugins can be added in controlled ways. In software, this makes it possible to evolve features safely over time; in documentation and knowledge systems, it supports adding new sections, reusable patterns, and additional use cases without losing clarity or maintainability. The goal is to keep the system adaptable, predictable, and easy to extend as needs change.
