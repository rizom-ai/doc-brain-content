---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the design principle of building systems so they can grow and adapt over time without major rewrites. It depends on modular architecture, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, loose coupling, and a stable core with well-defined extension points. A truly extensible system makes it possible to decide what belongs in the core versus what should be added as an extension, allowing new capabilities, integrations, or behaviors to be introduced in controlled ways without disrupting the existing structure. This supports long-term adaptability, preserves clarity, and reduces maintenance cost in both technical systems and other organized structures.
