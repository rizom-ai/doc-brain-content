---
title: Onboarding
---
Onboarding is the guidance and documentation that help new users, contributors, maintainers, or team members get started quickly and confidently with a project, product, or process. It is designed to create a smooth first experience, reduce confusion and friction, and make the path from first contact to productive participation clear.

Effective onboarding gives newcomers the practical entry points they need first: prerequisites, setup steps, access requirements, quickstarts, orientation, first-run flows, initial tasks, checklists, essential concepts, contribution guidance, examples, walkthroughs, and links to the most important resources. It should explain what the project is, how it is organized, what to read or do first, and how people should progress with minimal confusion.

Because documentation is often the primary entry point, onboarding should be easy to discover, concise, and structured to minimize the amount of prior context required. Strong onboarding turns a knowledge base into a practical guide rather than just a storage system, lowering the effort needed to understand the system and begin contributing.

Effective onboarding shortens the learning curve, improves adoption and retention, reduces support burden, and lowers the chance of avoidable mistakes. As part of documentation planning, it should align with workflow and priorities so the most important entry paths and early tasks are available when people begin.
