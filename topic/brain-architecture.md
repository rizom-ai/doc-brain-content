---
title: Brain Architecture
---
The structural organization of a brain system around a stable core, clear layers, and explicit modular boundaries. It defines what belongs in the core versus what should be handled through extension points or plugins, so the system stays understandable, maintainable, and scalable as it grows. Strong brain architecture emphasizes separation of concerns, low coupling, coherent knowledge organization, and well-defined interfaces for extension. By preserving a clean boundary model and a stable foundation, it supports long-term evolvability without requiring major redesign as new capabilities are added.
