---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparation used to determine whether a project, product, or system is ready to ship. It defines the conditions that should be met before delivery, linking planning with execution so milestones include not only what will be built but also what completion looks like. Readiness typically includes verifying required functionality, confirming quality and reliability, resolving dependencies and blockers, reviewing risks, and ensuring launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. Readiness reviews, checklists, and maintainer-facing documentation provide a practical gate between development and release, helping teams avoid rushed or premature delivery, reduce missed steps and operational risk, and make confident decisions about launch timing and scope.
