---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the ongoing process of organizing, creating, reviewing, updating, and maintaining documentation or other written knowledge so it stays accurate, clear, and useful over time. It includes structuring content, deciding where information lives, and using consistent labels, links, and groupings so contributors can find, reuse, and update material efficiently. It also covers documentation workflows and ownership, helping teams define how content is created, edited, and kept current as part of the development process. Good content management reduces duplication and inconsistency, supports clear navigation, and keeps project knowledge usable for both new and experienced contributors as the knowledge base grows.
