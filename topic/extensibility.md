---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the design principle of building systems so they can grow and adapt over time without major rewrites. It depends on modular architecture, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, loose coupling, and a stable core with well-defined extension points. In practice, extensibility means making a deliberate distinction between what belongs in the core and what should be delivered through plugins or other extensions, so new capabilities, integrations, and behaviors can be added without weakening or bloating the core runtime. A truly extensible system preserves clarity, maintainability, and long-term flexibility as requirements evolve.
