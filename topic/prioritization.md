---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of evaluating and ranking tasks, goals, ideas, or initiatives so limited time and capacity are directed to the highest-value work first. It helps determine what should be done next, what can wait, and how to make tradeoffs when demands exceed available resources. Effective prioritization turns broad goals and backlogs into a clear sequence of action by weighing factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, readiness, effort, and capacity. Using a consistent framework helps teams compare competing work more objectively, stay aligned on desired outcomes, and avoid scattered effort. It is especially useful for backlog triage, roadmap planning, release coordination, milestone sequencing, and other planning contexts where multiple possible directions must be balanced. In practice, prioritization helps teams focus on the most impactful improvements, defer lower-value tasks, and make clearer decisions about next steps when planning work or releases.
