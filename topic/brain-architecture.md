---
title: Brain Architecture
---
The structural design of a system around a stable core with clear modular boundaries. It defines how services, modules, and subsystems are organized and how they relate to one another so the system remains understandable, maintainable, and easy to reason about as it grows. Strong modular architecture emphasizes separation of concerns, a coherent core, and defined extension points, reducing coupling, isolating change, and preventing internal logic from becoming brittle or overly complex. It provides a foundation for extensibility and long-term evolvability by allowing new capabilities to be added without hidden dependencies or tight coupling. This is especially important in knowledge systems, where clear boundaries between core services and optional capabilities help organize content, logic, and responsibilities while keeping information storage, retrieval, and extension manageable over time.
