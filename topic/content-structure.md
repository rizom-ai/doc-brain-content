---
title: Content Structure
---
Content structure is the underlying arrangement of topics, sections, and relationships within documentation or a knowledge base. It defines how information is grouped, ordered, and linked so content stays clear, navigable, and reusable. Good structure creates a predictable hierarchy, reduces confusion and duplication, and makes related information easier to find and understand. It also supports long-term maintainability as the knowledge base grows by relying on stable organizing principles such as naming conventions, section order, relationship patterns, and reusable formats.
