---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of evaluating and ranking tasks, goals, ideas, or initiatives so limited time and capacity are directed to the highest-value work first. It determines what should happen next, what can wait, and how to make tradeoffs when demands exceed available resources. Effective prioritization turns broad goals, backlogs, and maintenance queues into a clear sequence of action by weighing factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, readiness, effort, and capacity. It is especially useful in project roadmaps, release planning, milestone sequencing, backlog triage, content maintenance, and choosing among competing efforts. In team and contribution settings, prioritization helps coordinate effort, reduce scattered work, and keep execution aligned with project goals and knowledge base health. Using a consistent framework helps teams compare work more objectively, stay aligned on desired outcomes, and focus on the most impactful improvements while deferring lower-value tasks.
