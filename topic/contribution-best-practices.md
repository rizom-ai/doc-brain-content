---
title: Contribution Best Practices
---
Guidance for contributing to a project, documentation system, or knowledge base in a clear, consistent, and reviewable way. This topic covers contributor expectations, standards, etiquette, and practical steps for proposing, preparing, submitting, and applying changes safely and predictably. It includes norms, conventions, and recommended behaviors that help new contributors understand what good contributions look like and align their work with project expectations from the start. It also covers code, documentation, notes, and other content contributions, along with workflow practices such as review, branching, local development, and coordination between contributors and maintainers. Strong contribution guidance should be easy for beginners to understand, preserve clean structure, reduce duplication, reuse existing patterns, and support navigation, future updates, and the broader system. The goal is to make participation explicit, improve quality, surface structural issues early, reduce confusion, and minimize coordination overhead.
