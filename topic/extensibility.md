---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so new capabilities can be added over time with minimal disruption to existing behavior. It relies on modular design, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, stable core abstractions, and intentional extension points or interfaces that create safe seams for future growth. The goal is to keep the base system stable while allowing new features or external components to integrate cleanly, reducing coupling and preserving maintainability as requirements evolve. Extensibility should be treated as a core design goal rather than an afterthought, with architectures anticipating future expansion and long-term adaptability across software, content models, and other evolving systems.
