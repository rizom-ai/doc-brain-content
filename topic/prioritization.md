---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of evaluating and ranking tasks, goals, ideas, or initiatives so limited time and capacity are directed to the highest-value work first. It determines what should happen next, what can wait, and how to make tradeoffs when demands exceed available resources. Effective prioritization turns broad goals, roadmaps, and backlogs into a clear sequence of action by weighing factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, readiness, effort, and capacity. It is especially useful in roadmap planning, backlog grooming, milestone sequencing, release planning, and choosing among competing efforts. In team settings, prioritization helps coordinate effort, make tradeoffs explicit, reduce ad hoc decision-making, and keep execution aligned with project goals and desired outcomes.
