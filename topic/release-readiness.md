---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is complete, stable, and safe enough to ship. It defines the threshold for moving from development into deployment or publication by making expected outcomes, quality gates, and delivery requirements explicit. Typical readiness factors include confirming that required work is complete, priorities align with the next milestone, risks and blockers are understood or resolved, quality and reliability expectations are met, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. Release readiness helps teams keep work truly shippable rather than merely finished, connects planning to execution, and provides a practical checklist or go/no-go checkpoint for reducing release risk and making delivery decisions more objective.
