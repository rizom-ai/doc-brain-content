---
title: Content Structure
---
This topic covers how documentation and knowledge are organized into a clear, reusable hierarchy that supports coherence, discoverability, maintenance, and long-term scalability. It focuses on the underlying shape of a knowledge base: how sections, pages, categories, and related ideas are grouped, sequenced, and connected so information flows logically from context to action. Effective content structure improves navigation and comprehension, reduces fragmentation, duplication, and inconsistency, and helps contributors understand where content belongs. It also provides consistent organizing principles, naming patterns, and relationships that keep the system adaptable as new material is added over time.
