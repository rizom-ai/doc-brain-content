---
title: Brain Architecture
---
The structural organization of a brain system around a stable core, clear layers, and explicit modular boundaries. It defines what belongs in the core versus what should be handled through extension points or plugins, keeping responsibilities separated and interfaces well defined. Strong brain architecture emphasizes separation of concerns, low coupling, coherent knowledge organization, and independently evolvable components. By preserving a clean boundary model and a stable foundation, it supports maintainability, scalability, and long-term evolvability without requiring major redesign as new capabilities are added.
