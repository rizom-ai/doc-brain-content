---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the ongoing stewardship of documentation and knowledge assets so they remain organized, current, consistent, and easy to reuse over time. It includes deciding where content lives, maintaining structure, labels, links, and conventions, and applying standards that keep information coherent as the knowledge base grows. A key part of content management is routine upkeep: reviewing, editing, updating, refactoring, versioning, consolidating, and removing outdated or duplicate material. This work helps identify stale content, prioritize what should be refreshed, reduce fragmentation and conflicting versions, preserve terminology consistency, and support clear navigation. Strong content management makes content easier to evolve without losing clarity or structure, and it keeps knowledge bases, documentation, onboarding materials, and other living systems trustworthy, durable, and scalable.
