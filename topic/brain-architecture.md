---
title: Brain Architecture
---
Brain architecture is the internal structural organization of a brain system and its markdown knowledge base around a stable core, clear layers, and well-defined modules. It emphasizes modular design over a monolithic structure, with explicit boundaries that separate core logic from optional extensions and plugin points. In a documentation context, this means organizing information into coherent, reusable sections with relationships that are easy for both humans and machines to understand. Strong brain architecture improves separation of concerns, low coupling, and knowledge organization, making the system easier to navigate, search, maintain, and extend. By defining how parts fit together and preserving a clean boundary model, it supports maintainability, reuse, scalability, onboarding, and long-term evolvability as new capabilities are added.
