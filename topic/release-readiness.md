---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is complete, stable, and safe enough to ship. It is a practical checkpoint in the planning and delivery process, not just a final approval step. Teams use it to assess completeness, quality, confidence, and scope alignment before a release happens, ensuring the work is implemented well enough to move forward. Common readiness factors include confirming that required work is complete, key conditions are verified, features are tested and meet quality expectations, risks and blockers are understood or resolved, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. A release readiness checklist helps teams apply these checks consistently and creates a more predictable go/no-go process. The topic is closely tied to coordination between development, QA, release planning, and support, and it should be built into the workflow rather than treated as an afterthought. As a final quality and coordination checkpoint, it connects planning decisions to deployment outcomes and turns prioritized work into a shippable result.
