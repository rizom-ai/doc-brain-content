---
title: Onboarding
---
Guidance and documentation that help new users, contributors, or team members get started quickly and confidently with a project, product, or process. Onboarding reduces first-time friction by making the entry experience clear, efficient, and welcoming. It typically includes prerequisites, setup steps, quickstarts, orientation, first-run flows, initial tasks, checklists, essential concepts, contribution guidance, examples, walkthroughs, and links to the most important resources. Good onboarding explains what the project is, how it is organized, and what a newcomer needs to do first so they can move from initial access to productive participation with minimal confusion. It should be structured to avoid overwhelming people, support different experience levels, and lower the barrier to entry. Effective onboarding shortens the learning curve, improves adoption and retention, reduces support burden, and helps people become productive sooner.
