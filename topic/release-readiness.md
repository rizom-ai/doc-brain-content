---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is ready to ship. It defines the conditions that should be met before delivery, creating a clear threshold for moving from development into deployment or publication and connecting planning with execution. Readiness typically includes confirming that required work is complete, risks and blockers are understood or resolved, quality and reliability expectations are met, key requirements are validated, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. Release readiness criteria make release decisions more predictable and objective by establishing clear completion standards, quality thresholds, and go/no-go gates. Readiness checklists and release reviews provide a practical checkpoint between development and deployment, helping teams avoid premature or ad hoc releases, reduce operational risk, and make confident decisions about timing and scope.
