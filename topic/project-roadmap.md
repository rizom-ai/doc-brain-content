---
title: Project Roadmap
---
A project roadmap is a high-level, time-based plan that turns strategy, priorities, and backlog ideas into a sequenced path of work. It organizes major workstreams, deliverables, milestones, dependencies, and release timing over a defined horizon such as a quarter or several months, helping teams decide what should happen next, what can wait, and how work should be staged for maximum impact. Roadmaps are especially useful when resources are limited or goals shift, because they connect near-term execution with longer-term direction.

The roadmap sits above detailed task management and focuses on prioritization, planning, and delivery sequencing. It uses roadmap items as input for organizing upcoming work and aligning it with project goals. A strong roadmap supports tradeoff decisions, coordination across product and technical efforts, and clear communication to stakeholders about scope, expectations, and timing. It should provide enough structure to guide commitments while remaining adaptable as priorities, constraints, and opportunities change. When tied to release planning, it defines milestones and shippable steps that keep the project moving steadily toward completed outcomes.
