---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so it can gain new capabilities over time with minimal friction and without major restructuring. It depends on clear boundaries, stable core abstractions, separation of concerns, and well-defined extension points or interfaces that allow future additions to integrate cleanly with existing behavior. The goal is to anticipate growth while keeping the core structure stable, reducing coupling, and avoiding breakage as functionality evolves. Plugin-based approaches are one way to implement extensibility, but the concept is broader and applies to technical systems, content models, and other evolving architectures. Extensible systems support predictable expansion, preserve clarity and control, and improve long-term maintainability as requirements change.
