---
title: Content Structure
---
This topic covers how documentation and related knowledge content are organized into clear sections, pages, and logical groupings so information is easy to find, follow, reuse, and maintain. It focuses on the internal shape of information: how sections relate to one another, how content flows from introduction to action, and how overall information architecture supports discoverability as the knowledge base grows. Strong content structure helps separate setup steps, contribution guidance, onboarding materials, and other guidance into predictable sections; it reduces duplication; improves consistency across guides; supports new contributors; and provides a scalable framework for clear, maintainable documentation.
