---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the ongoing stewardship of documentation and knowledge assets so they remain accurate, consistent, well-structured, and useful over time. It includes organizing information, deciding where content lives, and maintaining labels, links, and groupings so people can find and reuse material efficiently. It also covers workflows for creating, reviewing, editing, and updating content, along with ownership and maintenance practices that keep knowledge aligned with current reality. Good content management treats content as a living system, reducing duplication, preventing fragmentation, and supporting clear navigation as the collection grows.
