---
title: Content Structure
---
Content structure is the underlying arrangement of topics, sections, and relationships within documentation or a knowledge base. It defines how information is grouped, ordered, and linked so the system forms a clear, navigable hierarchy that supports coherence, discoverability, reuse, and maintainability. Strong content structure helps readers move from context to action, makes related areas easy to expand consistently, and reduces fragmentation, duplication, and inconsistency over time. It also relies on stable organizing principles such as naming conventions, section order, relationship patterns, and reusable formats so new material can be placed correctly as the knowledge base grows.
