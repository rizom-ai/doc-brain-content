---
title: Release Readiness
---
This topic covers the criteria, preparation, and checkpoints used to determine whether work is ready to ship. It includes evaluating completeness, stability, quality, and coordination to reduce risk and support predictable release decisions. Release readiness is a practical bridge between planning and delivery: roadmap items are translated into release-ready scope, with priorities sequenced to fit the current release window and items that are not sufficiently complete or feasible deferred. Rather than a one-time approval gate, release readiness is part of the development process, using milestones and readiness checks to confirm that work is intentional, coherent, and safe for downstream use.
