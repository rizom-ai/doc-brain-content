---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so it can be expanded with new behavior or capabilities over time without major restructuring. It relies on clear boundaries, stable core abstractions, and well-defined extension points or interfaces that let new modules integrate cleanly. A plugin-driven approach is one common mechanism for achieving extensibility, but the concept is broader than any specific implementation. Extensible systems reduce coupling, support future growth, and remain adaptable as requirements change, making extensibility a key goal for long-term maintainability and predictable evolution.
