---
title: Content Structure
---
This topic focuses on how documentation and markdown knowledge content should be organized into sections, pages, and logical groupings for clarity, reuse, and long-term maintainability. It concerns the internal shape of information: how sections relate to each other, how content is arranged for intuitive navigation, and how the overall information architecture supports discoverability as the knowledge base grows. Good content structure helps users quickly understand where information belongs and how to move through it, while also providing a scalable framework for consistent documentation practices and easier maintenance over time. The topic is distinct from maintenance because it addresses the structure of the content itself, not just how it is updated later.
