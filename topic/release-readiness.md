---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparation used to determine whether a project, product, or system is ready to ship. It focuses on confirming that the work meets minimum quality, stability, completeness, and safety expectations, that required functionality is present, and that blockers, dependencies, and release requirements have been addressed. Typical practices include readiness checklists, milestone reviews, and final validation to confirm alignment with scope, consistency, packaging, onboarding, and operational needs. Release readiness serves as a practical quality gate between development and launch, helping teams reduce risk, avoid shipping unfinished or misaligned work, and make confident, predictable release decisions.
