---
title: Brain Architecture
---
The internal structural organization of a brain system around a stable core, clear layers, and well-defined modules. It emphasizes modular design over a monolithic structure, with explicit boundaries that separate core logic from optional extensions and plugin points. Strong brain architecture creates separation of concerns, low coupling, and coherent knowledge organization, making it easier to reason about the system, change parts independently, and avoid cascading complexity. By defining how modules fit together and preserving a clean boundary model, it supports maintainability, reuse, scalability, and long-term evolvability as new capabilities are added.
