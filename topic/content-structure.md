---
title: Content Structure
---
This topic covers how documentation and knowledge are organized into a clear, reusable hierarchy that improves discoverability, comprehension, maintenance, and reuse. It focuses on the internal shape of content: how sections, pages, categories, and related ideas are grouped and sequenced so information flows logically from context to action. Good content structure makes information easy to navigate and understand, reduces fragmentation, duplication, and inconsistency, and helps contributors know where content belongs. It also supports scalable growth by providing consistent organizing principles, naming patterns, and relationships between content pieces that can be applied across a knowledge base over time.
