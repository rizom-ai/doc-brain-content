---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the ongoing practice of organizing, creating, reviewing, updating, and maintaining documentation or other knowledge assets so they remain accurate, clear, and useful over time. It includes deciding how content is structured, where information lives, and how labels, links, and groupings are used so contributors can find, reuse, and update material efficiently. It also covers documentation workflows and ownership, defining how content is created, edited, reviewed, and kept current as part of a maintainable system. Good content management treats content as a living system, reduces duplication and inconsistency, supports clear navigation, and helps prevent drift between documented knowledge and the current state of a project or system.
