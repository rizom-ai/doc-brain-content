---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is complete, stable, and safe enough to ship. It provides a practical go/no-go framework for assessing release-bound work consistently before deployment or publication. Common readiness factors include confirming that required work is complete, changes are tested and meet quality expectations, scope and priorities align with the release goal or next milestone, risks and blockers are understood or resolved, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. Release readiness should be built into the workflow rather than treated as an afterthought. It helps teams avoid premature launches, keep work truly shippable rather than merely finished, make delivery decisions more objective, and reduce release risk.
