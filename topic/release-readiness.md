---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is ready to ship. It defines the conditions that should be met before delivery, connecting planning with execution so teams know what completion looks like and when a release can safely proceed. Readiness typically includes verifying required functionality, confirming quality and reliability expectations, validating key requirements, ensuring milestones and tasks are complete, resolving dependencies and blockers, and making sure launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. Release readiness criteria make release decisions predictable and objective by establishing clear quality thresholds, completion standards, and go/no-go gates. Readiness checklists and release reviews provide a practical decision point between development and deployment, helping teams avoid rushed or ad hoc delivery, reduce operational risk, and make confident decisions about timing and scope.
