---
title: Brain Architecture
---
The structural design of a brain system around a stable core with clear modular boundaries. It defines how core services, subsystems, and optional extensions relate to one another so the system remains understandable, maintainable, and scalable as it grows. Strong brain architecture emphasizes separation of concerns, a coherent foundation, and well-defined extension points, reducing coupling and isolating change. This makes it easier to add new capabilities without disturbing the core design, preventing internal logic from becoming brittle or overly complex. As a central organizing principle, it supports long-term evolvability, especially in knowledge systems where clear boundaries between core services and plugins help keep storage, retrieval, and future expansion manageable.
