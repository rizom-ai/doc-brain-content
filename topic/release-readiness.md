---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether changes are complete, stable, and safe enough to ship. It provides a practical checklist or go/no-go workflow for assessing release-bound work consistently before deployment or publication. Common readiness factors include confirming that required work is complete, priorities align with the next milestone, risks and blockers are understood or resolved, quality and reliability expectations are met, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. Release readiness helps teams keep work truly shippable rather than merely finished, connect planning to execution, and make delivery decisions more objective while reducing release risk.
