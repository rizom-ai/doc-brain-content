---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of deciding which tasks, features, goals, or initiatives should be addressed first so limited time and capacity are directed toward the highest-value work. It turns a broad set of options into a workable sequence by ranking choices against factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, readiness, and available capacity. In planning and delivery contexts, prioritization shapes backlog grooming, roadmap decisions, release scope, milestone commitments, onboarding, setup, core development, and short-term execution planning. It is not only about choosing what matters most, but about selecting the right mix of work for a specific time window while balancing immediate needs with longer-term goals. Effective prioritization makes tradeoffs explicit, reduces decision fatigue and reactive planning, prevents overcommitment, and keeps execution aligned with business objectives and product direction.
