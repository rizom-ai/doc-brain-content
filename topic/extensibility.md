---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so new capabilities can be added over time with minimal disruption to existing behavior. It depends on modular design, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, stable core abstractions, and intentional extension points or interfaces that create safe seams for growth. A well-extensible system keeps the core stable while allowing new features, integrations, or components to evolve independently, reducing coupling and preserving maintainability as requirements change. Extensibility should be treated as a core design goal from the start, supporting long-term adaptability across software systems, content models, and other evolving architectures.
