---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so it can gain new capabilities over time with minimal friction and without major restructuring. It depends on clear boundaries, stable core abstractions, and well-defined extension points or interfaces that allow future additions to integrate cleanly with existing behavior. A key goal is to avoid breakage as functionality grows by managing dependencies carefully and keeping the core structure stable. Plugin-based approaches are one way to implement extensibility, but the concept is broader and applies to both technical systems and content models. Extensible systems reduce coupling, support predictable growth, and improve long-term maintainability as requirements evolve.
