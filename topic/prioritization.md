---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of ranking work, goals, or initiatives so limited time and capacity are directed to the highest-value items first. It turns a broad backlog into a clear sequence of action by weighing factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, readiness, and available resources. In product and project planning, prioritization guides roadmap decisions, backlog grooming, milestone sequencing, and release scope by clarifying what belongs in the next release and what should wait. It is especially useful for balancing competing requests and deciding between foundational work and feature delivery. Effective prioritization makes tradeoffs explicit, reduces uncertainty and decision fatigue, prevents overcommitment, and keeps execution aligned with goals and constraints.
