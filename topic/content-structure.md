---
title: Content Structure
---
This topic covers how documentation and knowledge content are organized into clear, maintainable sections, pages, and logical groupings so information is easy to find, follow, reuse, and extend over time. It focuses on the internal shape of information: how sections relate to one another, how content flows from introduction to action, and how overall information architecture supports discoverability and comprehension as the knowledge base grows. A strong content structure helps contributors navigate and maintain documentation efficiently, making it easier to understand where information belongs, spot gaps and inconsistencies, and keep content accurate. It reduces duplication and fragmentation, improves consistency across guides, and provides a scalable framework for readable, maintainable documentation.
