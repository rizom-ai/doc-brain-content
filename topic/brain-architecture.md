---
title: Brain Architecture
---
The structural organization of a brain system around a stable core, well-defined layers, and clear modular boundaries. It defines what belongs in the core versus what should be handled by extension points or plugins, so the system remains understandable, maintainable, and scalable as it grows. Strong brain architecture emphasizes separation of concerns, a coherent foundation, and low coupling between parts, making it easier to reason about the system and isolate change. By preserving a clean boundary model and explicit interfaces for extension, it supports long-term evolvability without requiring major redesign as new capabilities are added.
