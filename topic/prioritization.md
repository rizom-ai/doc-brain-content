---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of deciding which tasks, features, goals, or roadmap items should be tackled first so effort goes toward the highest-value work. It turns a broad set of possibilities into an executable sequence by ranking options against factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, capacity, and readiness. In planning and delivery contexts, prioritization shapes release scope, milestone commitments, onboarding, setup, core development, and roadmap sequencing. It is not only about choosing the most valuable items, but about selecting the right mix of work for a specific time window while balancing immediate needs with longer-term goals. Effective prioritization makes tradeoffs explicit, prevents overcommitment, reduces wasted effort on lower-value work, and keeps execution aligned with project objectives and product direction.
