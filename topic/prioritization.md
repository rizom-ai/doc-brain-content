---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of evaluating and ranking tasks, goals, ideas, or initiatives so limited time and capacity are directed to the highest-value work first. It helps determine what should be done next, what can wait, and how to make tradeoffs when demands exceed available resources. Effective prioritization turns broad goals and backlogs into a clear sequence of action by weighing factors such as importance, urgency, impact, strategic fit, feasibility, risk, dependencies, readiness, effort, and capacity. It is especially useful when planning releases, sequencing milestones, triaging backlogs, or choosing among competing efforts. Using a consistent framework helps teams compare work more objectively, stay aligned on desired outcomes, and focus on the most impactful improvements while deferring lower-value tasks.
