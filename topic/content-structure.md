---
title: Content Structure
---
This topic covers how documentation and knowledge content are organized into clear, maintainable sections, pages, and logical groupings so information is easy to find, follow, reuse, and extend over time. It focuses on the internal shape of information: how sections relate to one another, how content flows from introduction to action, and how overall information architecture supports discoverability as the knowledge base grows. Strong content structure helps separate setup steps, contribution guidance, onboarding materials, and feature or plugin documentation into predictable places; it reduces duplication and fragmentation; improves consistency across guides; supports new contributors and collaborators; and provides a scalable framework for readable, maintainable documentation.
