---
title: Content Structure
---
This topic covers how documentation and knowledge are organized into a clear, reusable hierarchy that improves discoverability, comprehension, maintenance, and reuse. It focuses on the internal shape of content: how sections, pages, categories, and related ideas are grouped and sequenced so information flows logically from context to action. Strong content structure makes documentation easier to read and update, reduces duplication and inconsistency, and helps contributors understand where content belongs. It supports consistent, navigable patterns that can be applied repeatedly across a knowledge base, making information easier to browse, refactor, and maintain over time.
