---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the ongoing practice of organizing, creating, reviewing, updating, and maintaining documentation or other knowledge assets so they remain accurate, clear, and useful over time. It includes structuring content, deciding where information lives, and using consistent labels, links, and groupings so contributors can find, reuse, and update material efficiently. It also covers documentation workflows and ownership, helping teams define how content is created, edited, reviewed, and kept current as part of the maintenance process. Good content management treats content as a living system, reduces duplication and inconsistency, supports clear navigation, and helps prevent drift between documented knowledge and the current system so the knowledge base stays reliable as it grows.
