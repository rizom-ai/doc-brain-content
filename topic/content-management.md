---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the ongoing stewardship of documentation and knowledge assets so they remain accurate, consistent, well-structured, and useful over time. It includes organizing information, maintaining labels, links, groupings, and conventions, and deciding where content lives so people can find, reuse, and extend material efficiently. It also covers routine upkeep such as updating, reviewing, editing, refactoring, versioning, consolidating, and removing outdated or duplicate content so the knowledge base stays aligned with current reality. Strong content management prevents drift between notes, preserves terminology consistency, reduces fragmentation and conflicting versions, supports clear navigation, and keeps new additions aligned with the existing information architecture. It applies to knowledge bases, documentation, and onboarding materials as living systems that need regular care to remain current, coherent, trustworthy, and scalable in collaborative environments.
