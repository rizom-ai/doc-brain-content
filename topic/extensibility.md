---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the architectural principle of designing a system so new capabilities can be added over time with minimal disruption to existing behavior. It relies on modular design, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, stable core abstractions, and intentional extension points or interfaces that provide safe seams for growth. A well-extensible system keeps the core stable while allowing features, integrations, and components to evolve independently, reducing coupling and preserving maintainability as requirements change. Extensibility should be treated as a core design goal from the start, supporting long-term adaptability across software systems, content models, and other evolving architectures.
