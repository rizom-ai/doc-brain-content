---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the ongoing process of organizing, creating, reviewing, updating, and maintaining documentation or other written knowledge so it stays accurate, clear, and useful over time. It focuses on how information is structured, where content lives, and how it is labeled, linked, and grouped so contributors can find what they need and reuse it efficiently. Good content management helps prevent duplication and inconsistency, keeps documentation aligned with the current state of a project or system, and supports scalable maintenance as the knowledge base grows.
