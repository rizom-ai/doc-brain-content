---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is ready to ship. It defines the conditions that should be met before delivery, connecting planning with execution so teams know what completion looks like and when a release can safely proceed. Readiness typically includes verifying that required work is complete, quality and reliability expectations are met, key requirements are validated, dependencies and blockers are resolved, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. Release readiness criteria make release decisions predictable and objective by establishing clear quality thresholds, completion standards, and go/no-go gates. Readiness checklists and release reviews provide a practical checkpoint between development and deployment, helping teams keep work aligned with shippable outcomes, avoid premature or ad hoc releases, reduce operational risk, and make confident decisions about timing and scope.
