---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether a feature, change, project, or version is complete, stable, and safe enough to ship. It functions as a practical quality gate in the delivery process, supporting a clear go/no-go decision before deployment.

A release readiness checklist is often used to apply these checks consistently across backlog items, features, or release candidates. Typical readiness factors include confirming that scope and required work are complete, verifying required conditions, ensuring testing and quality expectations are met, resolving or understanding risks and blockers, and putting launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities in place. Where external support is limited, the need for explicit, documented release discipline becomes even more important so missed concerns are caught internally before shipping.

Release readiness connects planning to execution and keeps development, QA, release management, and support aligned around launch preparedness. It is most effective when built into the workflow as a structured, repeatable process rather than treated as a last-minute approval step, because it supports reliability, predictability, clear communication, and reduced release risk.
