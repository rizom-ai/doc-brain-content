---
title: Documentation Organization
---
This topic covers planning and structuring documentation or knowledge bases so information remains easy to navigate, understand, and maintain as it grows. It includes grouping related material, defining a logical hierarchy and clear entry points, standardizing page layouts and naming conventions, and connecting related pages so relationships are easy to see. Strong organization improves discovery, reduces ambiguity, duplication, and fragmentation, and makes updates easier to apply consistently. It may involve reorganizing pages, defining categories, reshaping sections, or refining a documentation framework so the structure matches how people search for and use information.
