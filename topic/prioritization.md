---
title: Prioritization
---
Prioritization is the process of deciding what should be done first among tasks, features, goals, or roadmap items so effort goes to the highest-value work. It turns a broad set of possibilities into a realistic sequence by ranking options against factors such as urgency, impact, strategic fit, risk, dependencies, capacity, and readiness. In planning and delivery contexts, prioritization is used to shape milestone scope, release order, and roadmap sequencing while balancing immediate needs with longer-term goals. Effective prioritization makes tradeoffs explicit, helps teams avoid overcommitting, reduces wasted effort on lower-value work, and keeps execution aligned with project objectives and product direction.
