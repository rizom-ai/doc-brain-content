---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the design principle of building systems so they can grow and adapt over time without major rewrites. It relies on modular architecture, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, loose coupling, and a stable core with well-defined extension points. A truly extensible system is planned for change from the start: new capabilities, integrations, behaviors, or plugins can be added in controlled ways without disrupting existing structure. This applies to technical systems and other organized structures, where flexible interfaces and separable components reduce maintenance cost, preserve clarity, and support long-term adaptability.
