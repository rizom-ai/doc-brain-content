---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the organization, maintenance, and lifecycle handling of knowledge-base content so it remains easy to navigate, update, search, and reuse over time. It focuses on structuring information for clarity and long-term maintainability, including decisions about grouping pages, naming sections, and keeping related material easy to find. In a markdown-based system, durable knowledge is stored as typed entities with YAML frontmatter and markdown bodies, and those entities can be created, updated, searched, extracted, and synced through shared tools and workflows. The topic also includes keeping content current and consistent, managing publishing states and generation workflows, handling image references and prompt customization, and maintaining the entity graph that connects notes, links, posts, decks, newsletters, topics, and summaries. Strong content management keeps a knowledge base coherent, scalable, and reliable as a source of truth for both readers and agents.
