---
title: Onboarding
---
Guidance and documentation that help new users, contributors, maintainers, or team members get started quickly and confidently with a project, product, or process. Onboarding is an intentional pattern for creating a smooth first experience, reducing confusion and friction, and making the path from first contact to productive participation clear. It typically includes prerequisites, setup steps, access requirements, quickstarts, orientation, first-run flows, initial tasks, checklists, essential concepts, contribution guidance, examples, walkthroughs, and links to the most important resources. Good onboarding explains what the project is, how it is organized, what to read or do first, and how newcomers should progress with minimal confusion. It should be practical, concise, and easy to follow, supporting different experience levels while lowering first-time friction and helping people understand expectations and become productive sooner. Effective onboarding shortens the learning curve, improves adoption and retention, reduces support burden, and lowers the chance of avoidable mistakes. As a strategic part of project planning, onboarding should be connected to workflow, roadmap, and prioritization decisions so the most important entry paths and early tasks are available when people begin contributing.
