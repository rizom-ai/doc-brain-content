---
title: Content Management
---
Content management is the organization, maintenance, and lifecycle handling of knowledge-base content so it remains easy to navigate, maintain, search, and reuse over time. It focuses on structuring information to support clear retrieval, reduce duplication, improve readability, and make future updates simpler. In a markdown-based system, durable knowledge is stored as typed entities with YAML frontmatter and markdown bodies, and those entities can be created, updated, searched, extracted, and synced through shared tools and workflows. The topic includes keeping content current and consistent, managing publishing states and generation workflows, handling image references and prompt customization, and maintaining the entity graph that connects notes, links, posts, decks, newsletters, topics, and summaries. Strong content management makes the knowledge base coherent, scalable, and reliable as a source of truth for both readers and agents.
