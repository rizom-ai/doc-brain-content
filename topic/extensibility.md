---
title: Extensibility
---
Extensibility is the design principle of building systems, documentation, or knowledge structures so they can grow and adapt over time without losing stability or becoming brittle. It depends on modular organization, clear boundaries, separation of concerns, low coupling, stable core abstractions, and well-defined extension points that make future additions predictable and controlled. In software, extensibility means new capabilities, integrations, or behaviors can be introduced without major rewrites, often by separating core services from extensions. In documentation and knowledge systems, it means content can be expanded with new sections, reusable patterns, and additional use cases while remaining clear, maintainable, and easy to navigate. A highly extensible system keeps its core stable while preserving flexibility for future change, reuse, and evolving needs.
