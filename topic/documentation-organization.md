---
title: Documentation Organization
---
This topic covers how documentation or knowledge bases are structured and maintained so they remain easy to navigate, understand, reuse, and update as they grow. It includes grouping related material into a clear hierarchy, creating intuitive entry points and content maps, standardizing page layouts and naming conventions, and linking related pages so relationships are easy to see. Good organization also means placing onboarding and setup material where readers can find it quickly, helping new users move through the system efficiently. Well-organized documentation reduces duplication, fragmentation, and confusion, while improving retrieval, consistency, and long-term maintainability. It is especially important when multiple notes or guides need to fit into one coherent system, since structure directly affects usability and the ability to keep content reusable over time.
