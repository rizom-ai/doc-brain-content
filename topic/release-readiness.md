---
title: Release Readiness
---
Release readiness is the set of criteria, checks, and preparations used to determine whether work is complete, stable, and safe enough to ship. It serves as a practical quality gate in the delivery process, helping teams decide whether a release can move from development into deployment with acceptable confidence.

A release readiness checklist is often used to apply these checks consistently. Typical readiness factors include confirming that required work is complete, key conditions are verified, testing and quality expectations are met, risks and blockers are understood or resolved, and launch, packaging, documentation, and support activities are in place. The goal is to reduce release risk and ensure the product is ready for users.

Release readiness connects planning to execution and coordinates development, QA, release management, and support around a clear go/no-go decision. It is most effective when built into the workflow rather than treated as a last-minute approval step.
