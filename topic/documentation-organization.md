---
title: Documentation Organization
---
This topic covers how documentation or knowledge bases are structured and maintained so they remain easy to navigate, understand, and update as they grow. It includes grouping related material, creating a clear hierarchy and intuitive entry points, standardizing page layouts and naming conventions, and linking related pages so relationships are easy to see. Good organization improves information retrieval, reduces duplication and fragmentation, and lowers maintenance friction by making it clear where content belongs and how it should be updated. It is especially important as a knowledge base grows large enough that structure directly affects usability and consistency.
