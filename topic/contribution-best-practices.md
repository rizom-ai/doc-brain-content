---
title: Contribution Best Practices
---
Guidance for contributing to a project, documentation system, or knowledge base in a clear, consistent, and reviewable way. This topic covers contributor expectations, standards, etiquette, and the practical workflow for proposing, preparing, submitting, reviewing, and integrating changes safely and predictably. It includes the norms and repeatable processes that help new and experienced contributors understand how to work within project conventions, make participation explicit, and align their work with maintainers’ expectations from the start. Strong contribution guidance reduces ambiguity and friction, improves communication and review, avoids duplication, preserves clean structure, supports navigation and future updates, and helps scale collaboration through shared practices that produce predictable, high-quality changes. It also documents the preferred way to participate so contributors have a common reference point for behaviors, formatting rules, workflow norms, and quality expectations.
